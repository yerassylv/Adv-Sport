Safety First in Extreme Sports.

Extreme sports offer thrilling experiences and an adrenaline rush, but safety should always be your top priority. Learn how to prepare for your next adventure by following essential safety guidelines. Our tips will help you enjoy your favorite activities while minimizing risks.

Below here is some small instruction, full one you will get on the place.

Skiing

  • Speed Control, Adjust your speed according to the slope conditions. In foggy weather or crowded areas, descend carefully. Avoid cutting off other skiers.
  • Respect Others, Ensure your behavior and equipment do not endanger or harm others. Choose slopes that match your skill level. Respect lift lines and wait your turn.
  • Movement Path, You are responsible for those below you on the slope. Choose a trajectory that does not interfere with skiers below.
  • Stopping on the Slope, Do not stop where you are not visible to others. If you fall, move to the side of the slope as quickly as possible.
  • Overtaking: Maintain a safe distance while overtaking from the left, right, above, or below.
  • Starting to Move, Check the slope before starting. Look up and down to ensure it’s safe to begin or cross the slope.
  • Walking on the Slope, Stay on the edge if walking, and secure your equipment at all times.
  • Assist the Injured, Offer help if you witness an accident and call for rescue assistance.
  • Observe Signs, Follow signs and do not cross barriers. This includes lift boarding and disembarking areas.
  • Carry Identification, Quick identification helps in rescue and emergency procedures.

Skydiving

  • Pre-Jump Preparation, Attend all briefings and follow instructions from your instructor.
  • Equipment Check, Ensure your parachute and harness are properly fitted and functional.
  • Weather Conditions, Do not jump in adverse weather such as high winds or storms.
  • Landing Zone, Be aware of the designated landing area and follow guidance for a safe descent.
  • Communication, Maintain communication with your instructor or tandem partner throughout the jump.
  • Emergency Procedures, Familiarize yourself with emergency signals and procedures, including reserve parachute deployment.
  • Health Requirements, Ensure you meet the health criteria, including weight and medical fitness.
  • Post-Jump Debrief, Participate in a debrief to discuss the jump and improve future performance.

    • Paragliding

  • Site Selection, Choose a launch site appropriate for your skill level and weather conditions.
  • Pre-Flight Check, Inspect your paraglider, harness, and helmet before takeoff.
  • Weather Assessment, Avoid flying in turbulent weather or strong winds. Always check weather forecasts.
  • Takeoff, Use proper takeoff techniques as instructed during training to ensure a safe ascent.
  • In-Flight Awareness, Maintain awareness of other paragliders and potential obstacles during flight.
  • Landing Approach, Plan your landing early and aim for the designated landing zone.
  • Emergency Procedures, Know how to handle emergencies such as turbulence, collapses, or other in-flight issues.
  • Post-Flight Care, Properly pack and store your equipment after landing to ensure its longevity and safety.
